Polished recruiting profile with strong visual hierarchy, professional typography system (Oswald/Inter/Roboto Mono), and animated hero section with shimmer effects on athlete name. Live action photography in gallery section demonstrates athletic capability. However, missing strategic visual differentiation for a competitive recruiting landscape.

Finding 01 Asset Quality Gaps
Hero image is solid but single static shot limits narrative depth. Gallery contains 4 action photos but lacks environmental/lifestyle context. No split-screen comparison visuals (athlete vs. competition standards). Missing coaching breakdown video or form analysis imagery. YouTube film embed is present but lacks custom thumbnail or compelling CTAs. Athlete statistics table is functional but visually underdifferentiated from standard recruiting templates.
Finding 02 Mission Control Gaps
Analytics infrastructure exists (Google Tag Manager GTM-MPGDW4QD, dataLayer events) but no visible conversion tracking for coach inquiry actions. Contact section lacks email obfuscation or form submission—just raw email links. No visible A/B testing framework for layout optimization. Missing heat-mapping indicators or scroll-depth benchmarking. No evidence of recruitment funnel segmentation (D1 vs D2 vs D3 coach routing).
Finding 03 Analytics & Speed Gaps
Page load structure shows multiple font-family preconnects (fonts.googleapis.com) and deferred script loading, which is positive. However, YouTube iframe embed uses standard non-optimized embed code—no lazy-loading or performance tracking visible. No Web Vitals instrumentation evident. Film grain SVG texture and grain overlay (.grain div) add visual appeal but increase DOM complexity without performance monitoring.
Business Impact
Critical — Revenue Leak
What staying invisible is costing you.

Critical revenue gap: recruiter friction point at contact stage. Three separate contact cards (athlete/coach/parent) with mailto links but NO centralized inquiry form, no lead capture email verification, and no follow-up automation visible. Missing email verification or lead magnet (e.g., downloadable stats sheet, training log template). No phone number display for direct outreach. Potential coaches must navigate away to external email client—converting qualified leads becomes friction-heavy.

Recommended Action
The Photo-First Solution

Implement 3-layer visual strategy: (1) Replace single hero shot with animated photo carousel (4-6 race sequences showing progression/form) with Ken Burns micro-animation already coded. (2) Rebuild gallery as split-view comparison—left: action shots with right-side stat overlays (split-screen design reveals performance context). (3) Add embedded video timeline: create short 15-30sec reels showing 800m/1600m/XC form progression with on-screen PRs and dates. (4) Introduce 'Coach Scout Card'—high-contrast visual module showing key metrics (GPA 3.5, NCAA Eligible, 5'8", times ranked vs. state/national standards) with live comparison bars. (5) Replace generic contact links with embedded inquiry form with image-backed fields (photo + name field, photo + program field) to increase submission rate via visual association. Add post-inquiry confirmation photo carousel showing athlete's versatility across events.
