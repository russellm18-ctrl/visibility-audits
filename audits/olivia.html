Custom WebGL hero with kinetic typography and glitch effects creates premium visual intrigue, but the actual athlete photography is relegated to a horizontal scroll gallery with minimal visual hierarchy—missing the opportunity to showcase Olivia as the focal authority figure throughout the profile.

Finding 01 Asset Quality Gaps
Only 6 race action images in the archive section, all from single event (WIAA State 1600m). No lifestyle/training stills, no coach testimonial imagery, no facility/team context visuals. Hero section uses a procedurally generated brand mark (OD circle) instead of a hero portrait—photographer identity is prioritized over athlete brand building. Gallery images lack captions connecting to specific performance moments.
Finding 02 Mission Control Gaps
Three major contact cards exist but lack direct-to-coach workflow clarity. No clear 'request meeting' or 'schedule visit' CTA beyond generic footer button. Missing: video highlight reel link, athletic.net/milesplit embed, official recruiting questionnaire, coach feedback section, or parent contact option. Performance data is present but not contextualized against D1/D2/D3 standard benchmarks.
Finding 03 Analytics & Speed Gaps
GTM tracking is installed but no event-specific tracking for critical recruiting actions (email clicks, schedule views, contact form submissions). No heat mapping to identify which sections drive recruiter engagement. Missing: time-on-page metrics for media gallery, recruiter-source attribution, or conversion funnel from profile view to contact.
Business Impact
Critical — Revenue Leak
What staying invisible is costing you.

Russell Moore Photography uses this profile as a portfolio piece and lead-gen vehicle (footer CTA to 'Contact for Custom Profile') but captures zero direct athlete/family revenue. Olivia's family pays for the photography session but this profile itself generates leads for additional profiles, not for Olivia's recruitment outcomes. The site is a tool to sell more profiles, not to maximize Olivia's D1 recruiting potential.

Recommended Action
The Photo-First Solution

Replace hero WebGL with a full-bleed, high-contrast action shot of Olivia mid-race (leading, closing, or in championship moment). Create a 4-image 'hero grid' below hero showing: (1) race intensity, (2) coach/team environment, (3) academic/student life, (4) training/strength. Expand race gallery from 3 to 12+ images with captions linking each to specific PRs/meets. Add dedicated 'highlight reel' section with 30-60sec video clips. Move athlete portrait (headshot) into the vitals card section. This shifts visual authority from 'photographer's technical flex' to 'Olivia as D1-ready talent'—directly increasing recruiter engagement and profile-to-contact conversion.
