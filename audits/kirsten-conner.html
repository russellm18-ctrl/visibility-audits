Visual Authority Audit

Kirsten Conner

https://www.kirstenconner.com/
Visual Authority Grade
B

Premium Seattle interior design portfolio with strong visual hierarchy, sophisticated animations, and established press credibility—but project showcase relies heavily on image quality and narrative that feels underdeveloped for the caliber of work shown.

Asset Quality Gaps

Hero section uses only 2 rotating project images with limited context; project cards lack before/after comparisons despite having a dedicated B&A page; testimonial carousel shows only headshots without spaces/rooms being referenced; press logos are present but lack quantified impact metrics or case study depth; service cards are text-only with no visual differentiation or context imagery.

Mission Control Gaps

No structured project filtering by room type, style, or budget range; contact form is basic with no qualification questions to pre-segment leads; no clear pricing structure, timeline expectations, or service package definitions; analytics setup exists (Google Tag Manager) but no conversion tracking events visible for form submissions, CTA clicks, or lead scoring; no live project counter or social proof metrics (number of homes designed, years in business prominently featured).

Analytics & Speed Gaps

GA4 tracking enabled but no custom event tracking for hero CTA clicks, project card engagement, or consultation booking; no heatmap or scroll depth tracking visible; no form abandonment tracking; page uses Lenis smooth scroll which may inflate bounce metrics; no A/B testing infrastructure evident for CTA button positions, hero headlines, or testimonial display formats.

The Revenue Leak

High-intent traffic (project browsers, testimonial readers) lacks segmentation path to booking; consultation CTA is single external Calendly link with no pre-qualification; no lead magnet (design guide, inspiration lookbook, color consultation offer) to capture non-ready prospects; project detail pages don't load in provided HTML—potential friction point where interested prospects drop off; no email capture mechanism except contact form; social proof (12 press mentions, 5 testimonials) buried below fold on homepage.

The Photo-First Solution

Expand hero section to 4-6 rotating project hero images with location overlay text and immediate visual context ("Capitol Hill Kitchen Remodel" with 3-image carousel showing before/during/after); create dynamic project card gallery with thumbnail filters ("Kitchen," "Bathroom," "Full Home," "Contemporary," "Traditional") linked to detailed case study pages with 8-12 professional images per project; implement before/after slider in project cards; add room-by-room photo galleries to testimonial cards showing the actual spaces clients reference in quotes; create high-res press section with logo + publication + linked article + brief impact statement; build visual service cards with icon + example room image + linked service page; add Instagram feed integration (6-9 latest posts) above footer to drive social traffic and continuous content refresh.
