You're telling contractors you build for people who 'demand more than standard' — but your website is running stock photos tagged 'Extreme Sports Mountain Climbing.

Asset Quality Gaps

A significant number of visual assets are generic stock photos, particularly in critical areas like the hero section background, and the 'About Us' and 'Our Work' background images. This undermines authenticity and fails to showcase the company's unique projects. Crucially, several `alt` attributes are completely irrelevant to construction (e.g., 'Extreme Sports Bike', 'Extreme Sports Mountain Climbing'), indicating a copy-paste error from a template or a severe lack of content oversight. Some project images display work in progress (e.g., 'ADU Project in Progress,' 'Drywall Installation' under 'Painting' service) rather than showcasing finished, high-performance results. The theme mismatch, evident in 'awb-xsports' class names used for icons, further detracts from brand consistency and professionalism.

Mission Control Gaps

The glaring inconsistencies in `alt` text and the use of 'awb-xsports' class names point to a fundamental lack of brand governance and asset management. It suggests the site was built on a generic template without thorough customization or a clear understanding of SEO best practices and brand identity. The '2026' dates in image URLs (`wp-content/uploads/2026/04/`) are also unusual and suggest either placeholders not updated, or a disorganised approach to content scheduling and publishing, impacting perception of current activity.

Analytics & Speed Gaps

While lazy loading (`lazyautosizes lazyloaded`, `srcset`, `data:image/svg+xml` placeholders) is implemented, the presence of large original image dimensions (e.g., 2000px wide hero image) suggests potential for further optimization in terms of file size and responsive image delivery, which could impact Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) and overall page load performance. The `contain-intrinsic-size:3000px 1500px` for auto-sized images is an unusually large default, potentially affecting layout stability metrics like CLS if the actual image sizes are significantly smaller.

The Revenue Leak

The core revenue leak stems from a failure to visually authenticate the brand's high-performance claims. Prospective clients looking for 'custom' and 'modern' construction will be put off by generic stock photography and irrelevant image descriptions. This lack of genuine, high-quality portfolio visuals creates distrust and makes it difficult for visitors to envision Capitol Custom Construction executing their 'dream home build.' The inconsistent branding and lack of attention to detail in visual assets can lead to higher bounce rates, lower engagement, and ultimately, fewer qualified leads and lost project opportunities. The phrase 'We execute visions' is not adequately supported by the current visual story.

The Photo-First Solution

Implement a 'Photo-First' strategy by commissioning professional photography of all completed 'Capitol Custom Construction' projects, focusing on showcasing the 'high performance' and 'modern' aspects mentioned in the brand's hook. Replace all generic stock photos with these authentic project images. Immediately audit and correct all `alt` text to accurately describe the image content for both SEO and accessibility. Develop a clear visual brand guide to ensure consistency across all present and future assets, eliminating incongruent elements like 'extreme sports' themed icons. Prioritize showcasing the 'custom home construction' and 'ADU' projects with compelling finished shots. Consider adding project narratives or testimonials alongside visuals to enhance trust and demonstrate expertise, allowing visuals to lead the storytelling and firmly establish the company's authority in the custom construction market.
